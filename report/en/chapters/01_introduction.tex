\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

\subsection{Motivation}\label{sec:intro:motivation}

Research workflows in machine learning and adjacent computational
fields share a common skeleton: brainstorm a research idea, design
and run an experiment, analyse the result, write a paper, and
iterate. Each step is increasingly tractable for large language
models (LLMs), and published systems now automate substantial spans
of this loop. The AI~Scientist \cite{lu2024ai,lu2026towards} closes
the full idea-to-paper cycle and scores the result with an automated
reviewer; Agent~Laboratory \cite{schmidgall2025agent} executes a
human-provided idea under LLM-judged gates; AIDE \cite{jiang2025aide}
casts the engineering inner loop as tree search over candidate
solutions, the formulation MLE-bench \cite{chan2024mle} found to
dominate general-purpose scaffolds given the same model; VirSci
\cite{su2024many} shows that multi-agent deliberation produces more
novel research ideas than a single agent, stopping at the abstract;
Story2Proposal \cite{qian2026story2proposal} converts finished
experimental evidence into a manuscript under a persistent
structural contract; and PaperBench \cite{starace2025paperbench}
measures, post hoc, how faithfully an agent replicates a published
paper.

What none of these systems makes binding is the relation between
what the generated paper \emph{says} and what its experiments
\emph{produced}. Verification, where present, is advisory: the
AI~Scientist's reviewer scores finished drafts while hallucination
control stays at the prompt level --- its authors document
misreported hardware and a worsened metric presented as an
improvement, and name ``a more in-depth automatic verification of
the reported results'' as key future work \cite{lu2024ai};
Agent~Laboratory's simulated reviewers overestimate human reviewer
scores by 2.3 points on a ten-point scale \cite{schmidgall2025agent};
and PaperBench's central finding is an execution gap --- agents earn
substantial credit for written code, almost none for executing it
and matching the paper's results \cite{starace2025paperbench}.

ARI exists to close this gap. The user supplies a research question
and a compute budget; the system returns a publishable paper, the
experimental code behind every claim, and a complete audit trail:
every node of the search tree, every tool call, every model
invocation, and every dollar of API spend is recorded inside a
single \term{checkpoint directory}, the unit of reproducibility.

\subsection{Verified claims as a release condition}\label{sec:intro:verified}

ARI's distinguishing thread runs through the whole pipeline. At idea
time the run mints a \term{metric contract}: the falsifiable claims
the eventual paper must support, each naming the exact measurement
names that count as evidence. The contract is minted once and frozen,
because LLM naming does not survive regeneration
(\cref{sec:paper:contract}). During exploration, nodes emit
measurements under those names, the names steer expansion toward
uncovered claims, and \term{lineage chaining} lets evidence
\emph{computed} from earlier measurements --- a fit, its held-out
validation, a model-based selection --- execute along one lineage
instead of regressing to repeated probes
(\cref{sec:exploration:lineage-chaining}). At writing time, every
numeric mention is bound to the node and measurement it rests on,
and a deterministic claim--evidence gate --- no language model in
the loop --- re-derives each reported number from the recorded
results. Objective falsehoods block release at the final gate; the
subjective findings of a complementary semantic review only advise
the refine loop.

\subsection{Contributions}\label{sec:intro:contrib}

This report formalises four contributions:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item An \textbf{idea-owned metric contract} enforced by a
        \textbf{deterministic claim--evidence gate}
        (\cref{sec:paper:contract}): verification as a structural
        property of the pipeline, not a reviewer's opinion of its
        output.
  \item A \textbf{best-first tree search} (BFTS) over research-step
        nodes (\cref{sec:exploration:formalization}) whose selection
        and expansion are LLM-driven over structured candidate
        descriptions, with a deterministic fallback ranking
        (\cref{sec:exploration:node-selection}).
  \item A \textbf{paper-generation pipeline} keyed off a hierarchical
        rubric $\Rubric$ whose leaf judges grade individual claims;
        the system score is $\paperscore{\paper}=\sum_i \rubricweight{i}\,\judgefn{i}(\paper)$
        (\cref{sec:paper:rubric}). The PaperBench replicator is
        integrated as a tool call (\cref{sec:paper:paperbench}).
  \item A \textbf{checkpoint scoping} discipline: every external
        state (memory, cost ledger, lineage pointers) is written
        below \texttt{\$ARI\_CHECKPOINT\_DIR}, never the user's home
        directory. This is the operational consequence of the
        \emph{determinism} design principle, one of five that
        \cref{sec:overview:principles} lays out in full
        (we refer to it as P2 there and in the rest of the report).
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Scope of this report}\label{sec:intro:scope}

This report describes \texttt{ari-core} and the fourteen
\texttt{ari-skill-*} packages at version v0.9.0, whose theme is
end-to-end claim verification: the mint-once contract and gate
hardening of \cref{sec:paper:contract}; lineage chaining
(\cref{sec:exploration:lineage-chaining}); an opt-in ideation path
that drives the published VirSci deliberation engine unmodified over
a live literature snapshot (\cref{sec:related:autonomous});
platform-aware ideation, folding probed platform-capability facts
into idea generation so a plan cannot promise measurements the
execution platform does not provide; and a released sample paper
that is itself gate-verified, all twelve of its declared falsifiable
claims carrying machine-verified evidence (\cref{chapter:eval}).
These additions build on the v0.8.x foundations --- the
protocol-faithful three-stage PaperBench reproduction contract
(agent rollout, reproduction, grading), host-truthful environment
guardrails, and configurable search-evaluation layers; the
operational reproduction pass on a lossy-compression target reported
in \cref{chapter:eval} ran on the then-current v0.8.0 pipeline. The
body describes the algorithms and data flow at the module level; the
verbatim LLM prompts the system uses are listed in
\cref{sec:prompts}. The empirical study is preliminary at this
version, and a controlled benchmark is left to future work.
